Design overview

UConn HPSC is building a single-pilot, human-powered, free-flooded racing submarine focused on low drag, reliable systems, and repeatable testing. Our first build is designed as a modular platform—easy to assemble, service, and iterate as we learn.

Low drag
Modular systems
Repeatable testing
ISR / eISR focused

Timeline milestones

  • Concept + research: hull shaping, propulsion studies, rules/safety planning
  • CAD freeze: final hull/assembly layout + interfaces locked
  • Prototype phase: key mechanisms tested (fit, drivetrain, seals, controls)
  • Composite layup: mold/tooling → hull halves → trimming + finishing
  • Integration + pool testing: ballast/trim, steering response, safety drills
  • Iteration: refine based on test data and rebuild what needs improvement

Hull concept
Streamlined wet hull sized around pilot + SCUBA, designed for efficient flow and clean packaging.
Propulsion approach
Pedal-driven drivetrain transferring power to a rear propulsor (design optimized for reliability and easy servicing).
Steering
Rear control surfaces for pitch/yaw corrections, designed for predictable handling during testing and future race courses.
Safety features
Rapid egress plan, visible safety buoy system, and clear diver/pilot procedures as core design requirements.

Milestones

  • Completed: hull concept defined, subsystem layout underway, safety plan framework
  • In progress: CAD detailing + prototype parts/fit checks
  • Next: composite layup planning (tooling/mold), drivetrain bench test, wet testing plan and checklists
